Accounting & Bookkeeping
Track every euro in and out
▼
What you must do
Record every transaction — sales, expenses, invoices paid and received. Required by law.
How often?
Daily ideally, at least monthly. At year end, close your accounts and file your annual report.
Tools to use
Pennylane, Indy, or Dext connect to your bank and automate most of the work.
Need an accountant?
For companies yes — budget €100–300/month. For EI/auto-entrepreneur, you can do it yourself.
Pennylane → Indy →

Dividends — How to Pay Yourself
Take profits out of your company
▼
What are dividends?
When your company makes a profit, you can pay yourself some of that money as dividends.
When can you pay dividends?
Once a year, after approving your annual accounts at your AGM (general meeting).
Tax rate on dividends
Flat tax of 30% (12.8% income tax + 17.2% social contributions). Called "PFU" in France.
Salary vs Dividends
Salary = more charges but builds pension rights. Dividends = less charges. Most mix both.
Impots.gouv – Flat tax →
🧾
Taxes — What You Owe
Corporate tax, VAT, and more
▼
Corporate Tax (IS)
25% on profits. 15% for the first €42,500 if you qualify as a small business.
VAT (TVA)
Above €36,800/year, collect and pay 20% VAT monthly or quarterly. File online.
CFE
Local business tax paid every December. Varies by city and revenue.
Key deadlines
Corporate tax: 3 months after year-end. VAT: monthly/quarterly. CFE: December each year.
Business taxes – Impots.gouv →

Payroll & Social Charges
Pay yourself and your employees
▼
As a SASU/SAS president
You get a payslip and pay ~80% social charges on gross salary. €3,000 gross = ~€5,400 total cost.
As a SARL/EURL manager
TNS status — lower charges (~45%) but less social protection.
Hiring employees
Declare on Net-Entreprises.fr before they start. Pay ~42% employer charges on gross salary.
URSSAF → Payfit →

Invoicing — Get Paid Properly
Create legal invoices
▼
Mandatory info on every invoice
Your SIRET, client details, date, invoice number, description, price, VAT amount, payment terms.
Invoice numbering
Invoices must be numbered sequentially. Never delete one — issue a credit note instead.
Payment terms
B2B payments must be made within 30 days max by French law.

Money, Dividends & Taxes

How to pay yourself, optimize your income, and handle taxes without stress.

💰
Pay Yourself a Salary

Set a monthly salary. Even €500–1,500 builds your social rights. Must be voted in your articles.

📈
Mix Salary + Dividends

Small salary + dividends at year end. Best way to optimize your net income. Ask your accountant.

🏦
Keep a Cash Reserve

Keep 3–6 months of expenses in your account. Taxes and charges can arrive unexpectedly.

📊
Know Your Break-Even

Know exactly how much you need each month to cover all costs. Below this = losing money.

💳
Separate Personal & Business

Never mix personal and business money. Use a dedicated business account always.

🧮
Provision for Taxes

Set aside 25–30% of every payment received. Covers corporate tax, VAT, and social charges.

💸 How dividends work — simple example
Your company earns€100,000
Expenses & costs− €30,000
Profit before tax= €70,000
Corporate tax (IS 25%)− €17,500
Profit available for dividends= €52,500
You pay yourself €40,000 dividend
Flat tax on dividends (30%)− €12,000
✅ You receive net€28,000

Simplified example. An accountant will optimize this for your specific situation.

💡
Always work with an accountant

A good accountant (€100–300/month) saves you far more than they cost by finding the best salary/dividend split.
